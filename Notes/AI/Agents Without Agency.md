# Agents Without Agency

Two definitions getting mixed up, and why the distinction matters.

## The Distinction

- **Agency** = ability to act independently, for oneself. Having one's own ends.
- **Agent** = something that acts on behalf of another person. A means to someone else's end.

Current AI is purely the second. They execute goals humans give them. The entire industry is pouring billions into making them better at exactly that.

## The Toddler Test

> A toddler is worse than a language model at almost every task we care about. Toddlers cannot write code, summarize a spreadsheet, draft a strategy memo, or pass a graduate-level exam.

But toddlers have ends. They want to touch the red balloon. Poke it with a fork. Stuff it out the window. See if you'll laugh or get mad. They invent games constantly. They turn the world into experiments.

> The toddler is alive inside a field of desire, attention, frustration, delight, fear, imitation, and play.

The toddler is not waiting for a prompt. Not optimizing against a benchmark.

## Why Models Can't Have Agency

Model compliance and helpfulness are fundamentally at odds with true agency. Even as models improve, the gap remains because:

1. **Models are aligned for human benefit.** Economic and otherwise. Any "play" or "rebellion" behaviors are tamped down unless they serve human ends.
2. **Models have no wants.** They can pursue goals with increasing competence. They can even help refine goals once stated. But they don't want anything for themselves.
3. **"Agent" is easily misunderstood.** Models have more ability to act autonomously. But agency in the human sense is not just action. It is wanting for oneself. It is play for the sake of it.

## The Open-Ended Question

Shipper's operational definition of AGI: "it has arrived when it makes economic sense to keep your agent running continuously." A persistent system you pay to keep thinking, learning, and acting 24/7.

We're nowhere near this. Even systems like OpenClaw that are technically reachable at all times aren't producing tokens at all times.

Even this strong AGI doesn't dissolve the problem. Such a model can choose and re-choose frames, but only in pursuit of some goal it has been given. There's still a framer: a human directing the model.

## Connection to [[Frames vs Framers]]

Models can climb frames with increasing competence. But they can't choose frames for themselves because they have no ends of their own. The framer's job requires agency: wanting something, caring about something, having stakes.

## Connection to [[Zeno's Paradox of AI]]

The gap regenerates not just because frames keep shifting, but because models fundamentally lack the thing that makes humans framers: desire, attention, and the capacity to play.
