# Looking for a donkey

**Source:** Aeon  
**Author:** Aeon Video  
**Date:** 2026-05-20  
**Link:** https://aeon.co/videos/in-search-of-the-donkey-that-sparked-a-national-incident-in-venezuela

---

In 2018, two firefighters in the small Venezuelan town of Apartaderos posted a video online likening a local donkey, said to be named ‘Nicolás’, to then-president Nicolás Maduro. The gag quickly went viral, and the pair were swiftly arrested by counterintelligence police, leading to international condemnation. In _Looking for a Donkey_ , filmed six years later, the Venezuelan director Juan Vicente Manrique documents the incident’s local fallout while searching for the fate of the animal at the centre of it. In conversations with locals, Manrique establishes a fittingly surreal tone, capturing both the absurdity and the underlying tension of the situation. Along the way, the story that unfolds is often more anthropological than political, showing how, for many in Apartaderos, the connections between humans and animals run much deeper than the meme.
