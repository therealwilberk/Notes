# This is my island

**Date:** 2026-05-25

The idea of building a new community, off the grid and away from the world, is often associated with radical [utopianism](https://aeon.co/essays/why-the-communal-utopia-was-hard-work-for-its-children) or libertarianism. However, _This Is My Island_ , a 1972 episode of the BBC documentary series _Look, Stranger_ , captures a more conservative take on the impulse to start anew. The film chronicles life on Herm, a small island in the English Channel, which was leased by Major Peter Wood and his wife, Jenny Wood, in 1949. The Woods then set out to restore Herm, which had fallen into disrepair, and to build a like-minded, self-sustaining community from scratch.

Capturing the rhythms of life on this two-square-kilometre island some 20 years into the project, the film depicts a community where men labour, women care for children, and Major Wood presides over church services. In so doing, it charts the lives of people who have sacrificed many conveniences in exchange for a closeness to land and community. Herm was managed by Major Wood, and then by his daughter and son-in-law following his death in 1998, before the lease was sold to a trust in 2008.
