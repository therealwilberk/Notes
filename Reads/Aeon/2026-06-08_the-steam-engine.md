# The steam engine

**Source:** Aeon  
**Author:** Aeon Video  
**Date:** 2026-06-08  
**Link:** https://aeon.co/videos/how-an-ancient-curiosity-evolved-to-power-the-modern-world

---

In this short video, Bill Hammack, an engineer and professor at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, explores how the 1,600-fold increase in volume that occurs when water is heated into steam has been harnessed and refined by engineers over time. Integrating demonstrations, explanations and illuminating anecdotes, he traces the evolution of steam engines from novelties of the 2nd century BCE to the development of the modern turbine in the late 19th century, whose descendants still power much of the world today. The resulting presentation offers an enlightening glimpse into how scientific practice and knowledge, grounded in natural laws, can inform and simplify the ‘engineering method’, focused on practical outcomes.
