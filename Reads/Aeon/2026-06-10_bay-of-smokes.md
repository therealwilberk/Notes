# Bay of smokes

---

On 8 July 1943, there was something darker, denser and more caustic about the haze that often covered Los Angeles, California. Today, smog is considered an unwelcome yet frequent visitor, due to the city’s largely mountain-flanked geography and high vehicle emissions. However, on that Thursday in July, it was a novelty and a mystery – perhaps, many Los Angelenos thought, a Japanese attack on the homeland.

In _Bay of Smokes_ , the Los Angeles-based filmmaker Aric Allen weaves autobiography and local history to tell the little-known story of how just the right conditions set the stage for smog to arrive ‘like a stranded hitchhiker’ that day, engulfing the city in paranoia and, ultimately, contributing to a persistent public-health crisis. With his knack for immersive storytelling, Allen’s wandering account of Los Angeles smog unfolds into a skilful exploration of suspicion and unintended consequences.
