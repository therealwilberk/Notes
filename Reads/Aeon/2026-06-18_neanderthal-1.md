# Neanderthal 1

**Source:** Aeon  
**Author:** Aeon Video  
**Date:** 2026-06-18  
**Link:** https://aeon.co/videos/the-story-of-the-16-bones-that-upended-how-we-see-our-species

---

In 1856, quarry workers in the Neander Valley in the west of Germany stumbled on 16 humanlike – yet not quite human – bones that would upend our understanding of our species. The finding, which was made three years before Charles Darwin published _On the Origin of Species_ , ignited a series of scientific controversies and discoveries that still inform what we know about our humble place in the history of life.

This short from BBC Global visits the site of the Neanderthal Museum today, where a tower marks the spot where the bones of what would come to be known as ‘Neanderthal 1’ were found. Integrating expert interviews and fascinating anecdotes, the video traces the history of the site itself, the surprising amount we’ve come to understand about Neanderthal 1’s life, and what we’ve learned about our species in the years since these ‘16 bones that would rewrite history’ were discovered.
