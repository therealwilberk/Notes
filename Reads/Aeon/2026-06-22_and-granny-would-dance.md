# And Granny would dance

The Iranian-British filmmaker Maryam Mohajer is [known](https://aeon.co/videos/marmar-is-living-through-a-devastating-war-but-shed-rather-tell-you-about-her-new-dress) [for](https://psyche.co/videos/a-girl-upends-her-grandparents-love-story-in-this-hilarious-animation) animations inspired by her childhood in Tehran and rendered in her distinctive two-dimensional style. Drawn from ‘childhood memories and shared stories’, her celebrated short _And Granny Would Dance_ is told from the perspective of six-year-old Marmar, who glimpses a strange and fascinating adult world of stories and jokes shared between her grandmother’s friends over cards and tea. But Granny, who has been melancholic since her husband passed away, silencing the tuneful accordion he would play at home, struggles to summon a smile. Imbued with Mohajer’s knack for humour and her ability to capture the contours of childhood, the work makes for an unusually poignant and original reflection on grief, as well as a heartfelt tribute to the women of Iran.
