# Talk to strangers

**Source:** Psyche  
**Author:** Hannah Seo  
**Date:** 2026-05-15  
**Link:** https://psyche.co/notes-to-self/why-im-trying-to-talk-to-strangers-and-you-should-too

---

Last month, while clearing a drawer, I found a printout of André Breton’s ‘Manifesto of Surrealism’ (1924). The essay launched a movement that revolutionised art through beautiful, bizarre hybrids of words and imagery, like melting clocks and blue roses. Breton’s work emphasises how embracing randomness allows artists to bypass convention. Rereading Breton, I realised I’ve been implicitly following his guidance, not only in my own work but in my personal life too.

More often than not, the creative ideas I share with my bosses each day come to me seemingly at random – when I’m engaged in mundane activities like taking a walk or making dinner. I like to run with them, however bizarre or impractical they seem; in the past decade, I’ve shaped many such ideas into successful products and projects.

On the personal front, in law school, social circles rarely crossed year levels, but I remember feeling an apparently random affinity for a junior I barely knew. I took a chance on that connection and, almost 20 years later, we’re still best friends who chat every other day. When I met my husband in 2018, we were both active in the dating scene, yet we decided to move in together after just a few months. Our friends were sceptical, but we followed our intuition.

Maybe my comfort with randomness is a revolt against parents who demand order, or perhaps it’s simply a disinclination to walk the beaten path. Either way, I think embracing randomness is more important than ever as people’s lives are increasingly predetermined by packed schedules and overflowing commitments.

In his essay, Breton writes: ‘The mere word “freedom” is the only one that still excites me.’ I equate randomness with freedom. Yes, it can be risky, but it brings a certain levity to my endeavours, transforming every enterprise into a fun, inspiring, if slightly unusual, adventure.
