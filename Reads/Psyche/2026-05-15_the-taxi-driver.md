# The taxi driver

Dementia can have a profound impact on romantic relationships for both the affected person, who may experience changes in libido and desire, and their partner, who must navigate these new realities. The short film _The Taxi Driver_ features a husband coming to terms with his wife’s newfound hypersexuality, which she directs towards a fellow resident of her care home rather than towards him. Performed by the English actor Chris Connel, the monologue balances dry wit with emotional depth, offering a rare perspective on the day-to-day dissonances that couples who are dealing with dementia must weather, while raising complex questions about dignity and consent.

The short was created as part of _The Dementia Monologues_ series, which addresses ‘myths and misconceptions surrounding this disease’, from the Newcastle-based theatre company Live Theatre. The British playwright Fiona Evans based the story on the experiences of people with dementia and their carers whom she met during a series of workshops while researching a play.
