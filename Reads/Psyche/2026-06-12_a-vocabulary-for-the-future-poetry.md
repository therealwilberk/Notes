To build a better future, we must first conceive of it. This is the premise at the heart of _A Vocabulary for the Future_ , a series by the Centre de Cultura Contemporània de Barcelona, in which notable thinkers reflect on a word of their choosing and are paired with filmmakers who give their writings a visual interpretation. Together, they invite viewers to see familiar language anew.

In this instalment, the celebrated Romanian writer Mircea Cărtărescu offers his expansive definition of ‘poetry’, which he envisions not as a literary form, but as an egoless state of being that ‘belongs to the instant, free and inexplicable’. Cărtărescu’s writing is rich in metaphor and abstraction, and the Barcelona-based filmmaker Artur Tort and the Barcelona filmmaking collective Dostopos string together a series of dreamlike images that illustrate and often enrich his words.
