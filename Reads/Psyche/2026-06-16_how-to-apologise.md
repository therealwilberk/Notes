What separates a good apology from a bad one? As this TED-Ed animation details, authentic apologies are rooted in understanding the goal shouldn’t be your own relief, but genuinely seeking to understand the wronged party’s perspective and making an effort to repair any damage done. Using two distinct scenarios, the video draws from relevant research to highlight common apology pitfalls and explain why saying ‘I’m sorry’ is often just the first step. For more on the psychology of effective apologies, read this [Note to Self](https://psyche.co/notes-to-self/to-make-your-apology-convincing-use-these-kinds-of-words).
