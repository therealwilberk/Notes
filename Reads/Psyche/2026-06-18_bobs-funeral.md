‘It’s weird to have a family member you don’t love.’ These are the words the US filmmaker Jack Dunphy uses to open his acclaimed short film _Bob’s Funeral_ , before attending – and surreptitiously filming – the funeral of the family member in question: his paternal grandfather. From this premise, and punctuated by many a genitalia joke, emerges a darkly funny and poignant exploration of estrangement, trauma, grief and love. Dunphy explores these themes in his distinctive, idiosyncratic style, combining crude animation, lo-fi autobiographical footage and family home videos. In doing so, he arrives at a realisation that is ultimately quite profound: sometimes healing is a process that unfolds not just over years, but across generations.
