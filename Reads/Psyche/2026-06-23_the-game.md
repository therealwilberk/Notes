For all the athleticism, focus and years of training required to play football at the highest level, the most difficult job in the stadium on match day might just belong to the referees. While keeping pace with the players, they must be masters of the rulebook, and respond to the intense emotions of crowds and players with stoic firmness and humility.

In his observational documentary _The Game_ , the Swiss director Roman Hodel is granted rare access to the oft-overlooked workers who make the beautiful game possible on its biggest stages, including security staff, broadcasters and, especially, the man who could be considered the conductor of this unwieldy orchestra: the referee (in this case, the Swiss official Fedayi San). Tracking San from just before kickoff to just after the final whistle, Hodel’s film makes for a thrilling and immersive watch that is sure to give you a newfound respect for the most underappreciated person on the pitch.
