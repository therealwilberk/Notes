# Slop and Sameness

The economic engine behind why automation creates more expert work. Four-step cycle.

## The Cycle

**1. AI commoditizes human expertise.**

Models are trained on the visible residue of successfully completed tasks: code, prose, images, support tickets, product specs. They package yesterday's competence and distribute it cheaply to everyone.

Skills that used to be rare (coding a PR, making a YouTube thumbnail, writing a newsletter) become broadly available.

**2. Cheap competence gets adopted everywhere.**

Operations people issue pull requests. Marketers create YouTube thumbnails. Engineers write product guides. The volume of work explodes.

OpenClaw (open-source AI agent): 44,469 pull requests as of May 2026. Kubernetes got 5,200 in all of 2022.

**3. Abundance creates sameness.**

Everyone uses the same models trained on the same corpus. Output ranges from "decent start" to "plain slop."

> Slop is not any one particular mistake. It is not the use of em dashes, or a certain sentence rhythm, or purple accents on a landing page. Slop is visible sameness, repeated ad nauseam.

Slop is what gets produced by default when humans in many different circumstances use the same tool, trained on the same corpus, without thinking too hard.

**4. Sameness creates demand for difference.**

When everything looks alike, what doesn't fit the pattern becomes rare and valuable. Humans quickly learn to smell slop because the internet distributes it instantly.

> We demand not just any React app or research report — but one that feels exactly right for the person, company, and situation.

Demand for difference is demand for human experts. Because the current generation of models only knows about work that has been done. Humans know about what needs to be done, right now, at this moment.

## The Status Game

When new technology makes what used to be high status cheap, we invent new status games. When work is abundant and looks alike everywhere, the work that doesn't fit the pattern becomes the rare, valuable, high-status thing.

## The Expert Response

Human experts move in two directions:
1. **Build systems** that absorb the flood: review queues, evals, repo rules, CI, workflows that turn first attempts into great work.
2. **Do bigger work** using AI to reach levels impossible alone (like Calif finding macOS kernel exploits in 5 days with Mythos).

## Connection to [[The Allocation Economy]]

The allocation economy was the first-order prediction. Slop and sameness is the second-order consequence: more allocation means more output means more sameness means more need for expert judgment.

## Connection to [[Zeno's Paradox of AI]]

This is the economic version of the same paradox. The gap regenerates because cheap competence floods the system, creating new demand for the thing AI can't provide: situated, alive, expert judgment.
